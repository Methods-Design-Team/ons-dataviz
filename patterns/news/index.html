    How to display, tag and categorise news articles.

    When to use this pattern

    Use this pattern to publish a news article to a public-facing website.

    How to use this pattern

    News index

    This index sorts the already-published news so that the most recently posted article is at the top of the list.

    Use the articles component to build consistent summaries of articles.

    A list of categories appears on the news index, and you could also add contact details for the media team. Another optional feature is “Follow us” social media links to Instagram, Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn.

    If you do not include an image with each article, the news index will use a placeholder. Placeholders do not look professional or support the written content to tell a story. The articles component has advice on image dimensions.

            <p>Census Day was Sunday 21 March, but it is vital those who have not yet completed, do so as soon as possible.</p>

            <p>Sunday March 21 marked the day that millions of people across England and Wales completed key questions about themselves and their households to ensure local services in every community are informed by the best information possible.</p>

            <p>However, for those who have not yet submitted their online or paper questionnaires or have maybe misplaced their invitation letter, there is lots of help available.</p>

            <p>“The information you provide needs to be about who usually lives in your household on Census Day, which was Sunday March 21, however if you’ve haven’t completed it yet, please do so – there’s still time to,” the Office for National Statistics’ deputy national statistician Iain Bell said.</p>

            <p>“Every household should have received their letter inviting them to take part and we’ve had a great response so far. If you haven’t, or you have misplaced your letter, you can head online to <a href="#0">www.census.gov.uk</a> and request a new unique access code.</p>

            <p>“There is plenty of help available, including face-to-face assistance at local Census Support Centres.</p>

            <p>“Field officers will soon start calling at households who have not completed their census. They will follow social distancing and COVID-safe guidelines, supporting people to take part.</p>

            <p>“They will be equipped with PPE and will never need to enter anyone’s home. They’ll be operating much like a postal or food delivery visit.”</p>

            <p>The results from the census will shed light on the needs of different groups and communities, and the inequalities people are experiencing, ensuring the big decisions facing the country following the pandemic and EU exit are based on the best information possible, through the anonymised answers provided.</p>
